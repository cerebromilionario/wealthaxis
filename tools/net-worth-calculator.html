Frequently Asked Questions

What is net worth and why does it matter?

Net worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. It's the most comprehensive single-number snapshot of your financial health. Unlike income (which is a flow), net worth is a stock — it captures everything you own minus everything you owe. Tracking net worth monthly or quarterly is the single most valuable financial habit you can develop, as it reveals whether your overall financial position is improving.

What is a good net worth by age?

Common benchmarks: by 30, aim for 1x annual income. By 40, 3-4x. By 50, 6-7x. By 60, 10x. These are rough guidelines — actual targets depend on your income, lifestyle, and retirement goals. More useful: use the FIRE calculator to determine your target net worth based on your specific retirement spending needs, not age-based averages.

Should I include my home in net worth?

Yes, include your home's estimated market value as an asset and your remaining mortgage as a liability. Your home equity (value minus mortgage) is part of your net worth. However, be cautious about counting on home equity for retirement — unless you plan to downsize or rent, your home doesn't generate spendable income. Track liquid net worth (excluding primary residence) separately for retirement planning.
