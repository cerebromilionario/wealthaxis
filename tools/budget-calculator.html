Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 50/30/20 rule?

The 50/30/20 rule is a budgeting framework popularized by Senator Elizabeth Warren: allocate 50% of take-home income to Needs (housing, food, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments), 30% to Wants (dining out, entertainment, vacations, subscriptions), and 20% to Savings & Debt Repayment (emergency fund, retirement, investing, extra debt payments). It's a starting point — adjust ratios based on your income, location, and goals.

What counts as 'needs' vs 'wants'?

Needs are expenses you cannot reasonably avoid: rent/mortgage, groceries, utilities, health insurance, transportation to work, and minimum loan payments. Wants are discretionary: restaurants, streaming services, gym memberships (unless medically prescribed), travel, entertainment, and upgrades beyond the basic need. When in doubt, ask: 'Could I live without this for 6 months?' If yes, it's likely a want.

I can't hit 20% savings — what do I do?

Start with whatever you can — even 5% is better than 0%. Prioritize: (1) employer 401(k) match (free money), (2) emergency fund to $1,000, (3) high-interest debt payoff, (4) max Roth IRA, (5) max 401(k). Increase your savings rate by 1% every 3 months. Most people find this gradual increase painless while dramatically improving their long-term financial position. At a 20% savings rate, you're on track to retire in roughly 37 years from scratch.
