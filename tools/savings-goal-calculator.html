Frequently Asked Questions

What return rate should I use for a savings goal?

For short-term goals (under 3 years), use 0-2% (high-yield savings or CDs — don't risk the principal). For medium-term (3-7 years), use 4-6% (conservative bond/stock mix). For long-term goals (7+ years), use 6-8% (diversified stock portfolio). The key rule: the shorter your timeline, the more conservative your expected return should be.

How do I calculate monthly savings needed for a goal?

The formula: Monthly payment = (Goal - Current × (1+r)^n) / [((1+r)^n - 1) / r], where r is the monthly rate and n is the number of months. This calculator handles it automatically — just enter your target amount, current savings, return rate, and deadline to find the required monthly contribution.

What is a good emergency fund savings goal?

Most financial experts recommend 3-6 months of essential expenses in a liquid emergency fund. If your monthly expenses are $4,000, aim for $12,000-$24,000. Keep emergency funds in high-yield savings accounts earning 4-5% — don't invest them in stocks where short-term volatility could mean the money isn't available when you need it.
